Bzzz bzzz.... it's the bleep and i've got a ward call for you

Here, you can find a collection of commonplace scenarios frequently encountered in the hospital setting. Our aim is to provide you with insights that will not only familiarize you with these situations but also empower you for a more confident approach on the wards. Please note these are by no means exhaustive or to be used as clinical guidlines.

Scenario:
You're called to a patient with chest pain. Follow the A-E approach:

A - Airway: Ensure a patent airway. Address any signs of compromise.

B - Breathing: Check respiratory rate, chest movement, and administer oxygen as needed.

C - Circulation: Assess pulse, blood pressure. Start fluids if hypotensive.

D - Disability: Brief neuro assessment for consciousness and neurological function.

E - Exposure/Environment: Undress for a focused chest examination.

Foundation Training Tips

What is Foundation Training?

In a nutshell this a 2 year programme where you will rotate between six different specialties within two hospitals. These rotations are ones you will choose yourself through a ranking system. This ranking system opens towards the end of medical school and allowd you to choose which sets of rotations, and locations, are more suited to yourself. The ranking system has changed from points-based where whoever gets the highest rank gets first pick to a system of "best-fit".This will try to allocate everyone to one of their top choices. This reduces the liklihood of being sent somewhere really low down on your list.

What are the requirements?

So, you've graduated medical school and relieved about finally being done with all those tick boxes! Sorry, you're not done yet, don't panic though because they're not that bad.

Within the UK Foundation Programme (UKFPO) you must show that you're a safe and professional member of the healthcare workforce.

This is done through demonstrating core outcomes:

  1. Patient care
  2. Integration into the workforce
  3. As a professional
  4. See more by visiting the UKFPO Website here.

What you need to do:

  1. SLEs e.g. miniCEX and DOPS. The 2021 curriculum states that there are not a specific number required however you need enough evidence to demonstratae capabilities. Note: your foundation school may suggest you do a certain number e.g. minimum 5 mini-CEX.
  2. Reflections. Reflect on events/cases/scenarios you encounter.
  3. TAB akak multisource feedback. You will be required to do this to demontsrate that you are valued member of the team. This involves you asking for feedback from many members of the MDT. It's not too honourous as long as you give people enough time and collect emails early.
  4. Placement Supervision Group (PSG) is a form that senior staff e.g. Consultants in your specific rotation will complete a form which will help inform your clinical supervisor end of placement report.
  5. Self-directed learning: Attendance on modules or teaching that has value to you.
  6. PSA The prescribing safety assessment is a summative examination in F1 that you must pass.
  7. Supervisior reports. At the end of each attachment you will meet with your Clinical supervisor and educational supervisior to discuss how the placement has been, any concerns and your portfolio progress. They will compare your evidence against what is required before making a decision about whether you have either: unsasisfactory, satisfactory or exceptional performance. In this case Satisfactory is good.
  8. Foundation Teaching: You are required to attend 30 hours. This may be delivered in house e.g Wednesday afternoons. This can be logged in your Personal Learning Log.

What shifts will I work?

Shift work can vary depending on the specific job you rotate into. Generally, the breakdown is something like below but it's not exhaustive:
Day Shift
Usually 9am-5pm +/- 1 or 2 hours
Twilight
Examples include: 2pm-10pm, 4pm-12am
On call
Usually a 12 hour shift where you take referrals from 8am-8pm or 8pm-8am.
Night Shift
8pm-8am where you will either cover wards (possibly with crash bleep) or are on-call.
Post take ward round
This is specific to surgery after an on-call shift the team will meet earlier to go through patients admitted overnight. Start times vary e.g. 7am-5pm shift.
Weekend
This can vary depending on responsiblities. Likely 8am-8pm ward cover or 8pm-8am ward cover nights.

When can I go on holiday?

Unfortunately, you cannot take leave whenever you want. This will depend on employers however it is harder to take leave on twlightlight shifts, night shifts and weekend shifts due to cover. Normally, you'll have to do some shift swaps with colleagues to get these days off. You can take leave on normal days e.g. 9am-5pm dependent on ward cover. If it is only you on the ward sometimes the rota co-ordinators will not allow it as there might not be any cover. For example, lets say you're working Mon-Thursday 9am-5pm but are scheduled for weekend cover on Sat-Sun (with the following Mon off). You can take leave on the Mon-Thurs however you will have to organise a swap with a colleague for the weekend shifts. Swaps can be a challenge, the rotas are pre-made before you arrive however if you ask colleagues early there's bound to be someone who might do a swap, with you covering a shift for them on another date. Plan early if you can. That said it can be hard to organise leave more than four months into the future as you will rotate every 4 months, also rotating co-ordinators. Every 4 months you will get an average of 9 days leave that you should use within that 4 month block. It is difficult to transfer leave and only done in exceptional circumstances that have to be agreed by seniors, rota co-ordinators and possibly educational/clinical supervisors.

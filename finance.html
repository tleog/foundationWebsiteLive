Understanding Finances

For many of you working as a foundation doctor year 1 will be your first job and that means first pay check! As this is exciting, it is easy to overlook key details. What we'll do is break-down an example of a paycheck to make it less daunting.

When do I get paid?

It varies from trust to trust for your standard employment but expect it at the end of the month. Some trusts will pay on the last working day whereas others will have a fixed date e.g. 25th of each month. For locum (bank) work it may be paid out weekly or fortnightly. This can be delivered via an e-payslip or a physical letter.

What will my salary be?

This varies for a few reasons. To understand monthly take home we need to understand how pay varies with the job. All Foundation Doctors will be paid a "base rate". Doctors who take part in additional hours above standard 40 hours, night shifts, weekend cover.

Paycheck explained

Imagine it's pay day and you log into your e-payslip to find this. It's a bit overwhelming but we'll break it down step-by-step.

  • This is an overview of your entire pay slip. Refer to this as we break down each section.

  • 1. Top section

    1. Employee Name
    2. Where you work
    3. What your job is
    4. Your annual (12-month total) salary
    5. The average hours worked (Standard Hrs)
    6. Your Tax code
    7. Your local tax office code
    8. National Insurance Number

  • 2. Payments

    1. Hours worked
    2. Hourly rate
    3. Additional hours (Addn) worked over 40 hours
    4. Night shift rate which is 37% of standard rate
    5. Weekend rate. This example shows a rate for less than 1 in 3 weekends worked

  • 3. Deductions

    1. PAYE - Pay-As-You-Earn income tax
    2. NIA - National Insurance Contributions
    3. NHS pension deduction (you can opt out)
    4. Student loan repayments
    5. Other deductions e.g. Doctors Mess Fees

  • 4. Balances and Month Summary

    1. On the left appear cumulative balances to date
    2. On the right you can see a monthly summary of gross (before deductions) and net pay (what you get paid)
    3. Payment method - BACS is basically bank transfer

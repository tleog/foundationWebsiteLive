Medical Apps

British National Formulary

This app is a fundamental when you want to look up drug doses, contraindications or clinical guidance. I think it's much better than buying a physical copy every 6 months...

BMJ Best Practice

Simple app, brilliant for an overview of most medical conditions including history, investigation and management.

MDCalc

MDCalc provides clinical decision support tools and calculators. This app speeds up looking up scoring systems, allowing you to work safely and efficiently.
Generic Clinical Apps

Steth-o-cope

Late-night ward calls? Not sure what to do? Don't panic and read this guide.

Accurx Switch

If you need a number, here's your directory! This app has commonly used phone numbers and is regularly updated by the user base allowing you to skip out the switchboard!

Surgical Apps

Teach Me Anatomy

Great app for a quick anatomy reminder or exam revision with its sections detailing core regions of clinical significance.

Teach Me Surgery

Similar to Teach Me Anatomy however with a greater focus on key surgical anatomy, conditions and procedures. I found this very useful during my General Surgery rotation.

OrthoFlow

A very handy guide for common fracture management. I have found this app incredibly useful during my time in Trauma & Orthopedics.

Orthobullets

This app gives clear and coherent information (it's in bullet points!) surrounding orthopaedic conditions.
Acute Care Apps

Toxbase

This app gives you rapid guidelines for managing the most common overdoses.

iResus

Easy to access guidance from the Resus Council including adult, paediatric and neonatal guidelines.
Revision Apps

BMJ On Exam

BMJ OnExamination offers medical exam preparation with practice questions, mock exams, and revision resources for various medical specialties.

Osmosis

This is a medical education platform offering video lectures, interactive case studies, and study materials for medical students and professionals.

Passtest

A regularly updated question bank, great for medical schools examinations and post-graduate exams alike.

GeekyMedics

Founded by Dr. Lewis Potter, provides medical education resources including clinical skills videos, OSCE guides, and revision materials. This is a resource that many find useful typically when revising for medical school OSCEs.
